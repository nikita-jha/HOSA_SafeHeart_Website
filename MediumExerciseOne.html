SafeHeart: Medium Risk Exercise

Exercise Plan for Medium Risk Patients

    For people who are not in immediate danger for heart attacks, but their levels still deviate significantly from the normal amount, we suggest that the patient consult a doctor to ensure that a heart attack has not already occured. If it is has occured, they should pursue treatment as prescribed; however, if a heart attack has not occured, we recommend a series of semi-high intensity workouts focused on building a strong cardiovascular system by exercising 45 to 90 minutes a day. These exercises should substantially raise the patinet's heart rate, causing strengthening of the heart and blood vessels and improving the flow of oxygen in the process.

    Suggestions for this include:

    • Cross Country Running
    • Burpees
    • Jump Squats
