SafeHeart: High Risk Exercise

Exercise Plan for High Risk Patients

If the patient's levels deviate substantially from normal, he/she should proceed to a hospital immediatly to ensure that a heart attack has not already occured. If a heart attack has occured, they should follow the course of treatment prescribed by the doctor; however, if a heart attack has not yet occured, we suggest that drastic measures, such as investing in machines or gym memberships, be taken to ensure good health. Exercises on this level should be performed around 60 to 120 minutes a day, with periods of rest that are equal, if not longer, than the periods of excerise. We stress that rest is equally important to exercise when pursuing a strict regime.

Suggestions for this include:

  • Triathlons
  • Standing Pike
  • Planks
  • Cardio machines such as treadmills and ellipticals

If these workouts seem too monotonous or tedious, another form of exercise that not only strengthens the cardiovascular system but is also provocative is participating in sports, such as:

  • Basketball
  • Tennis
  • Marathon Swimming
  • Soccer
  • Lacrosse
